What is Basketball?

Basketball is a physical sport played between 2 teams of 5 players (+ substitute players) each on a retangular court. Each team scores by tossing the ball through the opponent's goal, which is a hoop that is usually 10 feet high with a rectangular board against it. The players have 4 quarters (usually 10 minutes each) to score as many points as possible. The team with the most points by the end of the game wins.

History Of Basketball



Where it all began

On the winter of 1891, Dr. James Naismith who was a Canadian-American physical educator for Springfield College in Massachusetts, wanted to come up with a new indoor athletic activity to occupy his physical education class during the cold winter. He spent 2 weeks doing so in his office. He first started with a combination of other popular sports at that time such as rugby, soccer and lacrosse, however, they often led to his students getting injured.

He then went to find two peach baskets and nailed them to a railing of the gymnasium balcony, one at each end, which so happened to be 10 feet tall. Since the peach baskets did not have holes at the bottom at that time, someone had to stand at the top of the balcony to retrieve the ball every time it gets shot into the basket. With some basic rules implemented, the first game of basketball was played. Decades later, it is now a worldwide sport.

The Offense and Defense

Basketball is a game where 2 teams take turns playing offense and defense, depending on which has possesion of the ball.

Offense:

The team with the ball are the offensive players. Their goal is to score points by shooting the ball into the opposing team's basket. Offensive players are the only one who can score points. players dribble and pass the ball strategically in order to get an open shot.

Defense:

The team without the ball are the defensive players. Their goal is to stop the opposing team from scoring the ball in their basket, and retrieve the ball to gain possesion.

Basic Violations

Violations are rules implemented to turn the possession over. There are many violations in the game of basketball, but here are the more common one

Time Violation

Offensive players are given 24 seconds, known as a shot clock, to score the ball. This shot clock only starts counting down after the offensive team gains possession. If the time runs out before the offesinve team attempts to score a basket, the possession flips.

Dribbling Violations

This includes:
- Travelling: When a player makes three steps whilst holding the ball with two hands or against their body
- Carrying: When a player holds the ball with their hand underneath the ball and then continues to dribble the ball
- Double Dribble: When a player uses both hands to dribble the ball
- Kicking the ball: When a player kicks the ball

Foul Violations

These violations are slightly more serious as it results the player from gaining a personal foul. If a player has 5 (or 6 in NBA rules) personal fouls, they are out for the rest of the game. These violations are given out if a player comes into contact with another in a physical manner. The person who gets fouled may also receive a free throw.

Positions

There are 5 main positions in basketball

Point Guard:

The point guard, also known as the "One" position, is the typically the player with the best ball handling skills and passer. They are usually also the fastest and good a driving in the basket. They are in charge of handling the ball and calling plays during the game.

Shooting Guard:

The shooting guards, also known as the "Two" position, are players who are the best shooters in the team. Their main objective is to score points for the team.

Small Forward:

Small forwards, or the "Three" position, are like shooting guards but more versatile. They are usually the most athletic in the team who can score from a range and also in the paint.

Power Forward:

Power forwards, or the "Four" position, play a big part for both offense and defense as they have to help make plays for the point guard on offesne, and defend the under the basket and grab rebounds on defense.

Center:

Centers, or the "Five" position, are usually the biggest player in the team. Just like power forwards, their job is to grab rebounds and defend the under basket.

Kobe Bean Bryant

Kobe Bryant was a professional NBA player (1996 - 2016). He played as a guard for the Los Angelos Lakers for all 20 years of his NBA career. Throughout those years he has been an influence to many basketball players alike. He was widely celebrated as one of basketball's all-time greats. Kobe has won five NBA championships. He has also won 2 Gold Olympic medals for the United States Men's team in 2008 and 2012. What was most admirable about him was his work. He has sadly passed away at the age of 41 due to a helicopter crash. His daughter, Gianna Bryant, 13 at that time, was also in the helicopter and sadly passed as well.

Michael Jeffrey Jordan

Michael Jordan, age 60, is a retired NBA player (1984 - 2003) who mostly played for the Chicago Bulls as a shooting guard and small forward. Jordan changed the game of basketball through his superior skills, high flying athleticism and determination to win at all cost. Throughout his career he has won 6 NBA championships and 2 Gold Olympic Medals with the United States Men's team in 1984 and 1992.

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

Kareem, age 76, was a NBA player back in 1969 to 1989. He played for the Milwaukee Bucks and Los Angelos Lakers. He played as a Center who dominated in his time. As a 7 foot tall player, it was almost impossible to stop him from scoring in the paint. His unstoppable signature shot, the "Skyhook", helped him score a total of 38,387 points, the most in the NBA before recently broken by Lebron James. Additionally, he has won 6 NBA championships.
